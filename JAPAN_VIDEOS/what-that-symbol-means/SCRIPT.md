# What That Symbol on Your Container Means - Full Script

**Platform:** Instagram Reels (@PolystyreneGuy)
**Target Length:** 45-60 seconds
**Tone:** Educational, casual, "bet you didn't know this" energy

---

**[HOOK - First 2 seconds]**

[Hold up a takeout container, bottom facing camera]

"You've seen this symbol a thousand times and you probably have no idea what it means."

**[Beat 1 - The Symbol]**

[Point to the recycling triangle with the number 6]

"This little triangle with a 6 inside it? That's a resin identification code. And the number 6 means this container is made of polystyrene."

**[Beat 2 - What the Numbers Mean]**

[Cut to b-roll of different containers]

"Every plastic product has one of these codes, numbered 1 through 7. Number 1 is PET, that's your water bottles. Number 2 is HDPE, like milk jugs. [pause] Number 6, polystyrene, shows up on foam cups, takeout containers, egg cartons, and meat trays."

**[Beat 3 - Why It Matters]**

[Back to camera]

"Here's why this matters. When you know the number, you know the material. When you know the material, you know how to recycle it. Most people just see a triangle and assume it means recyclable. But that triangle isn't a recycling symbol. It's an identification code."

**[Beat 4 - The Action Step]**

[Hold up the container again]

"So next time you're about to toss a container, flip it over. Look for the number. If it says 6, that's polystyrene, and in many areas it can be recycled if you bring it to the right place."

[Direct to camera, slight smile]

"Now you know. Follow for more."

**[END]**

---

**Speaker Notes:**
- Make the hook feel like you're letting them in on a secret
- When listing the numbers, keep it brisk, don't turn it into a lecture
- Emphasize "that triangle isn't a recycling symbol" with a slight pause before it
- End with confidence, not a hard sell

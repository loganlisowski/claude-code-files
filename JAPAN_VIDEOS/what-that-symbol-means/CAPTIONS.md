# What That Symbol on Your Container Means - Caption Variations

---

## Caption 1

That triangle on the bottom of your takeout container? It's not a recycling symbol. It's a resin identification code. The number inside tells you what type of plastic it's made from. Number 6 means polystyrene, the material in foam cups, egg cartons, and meat trays. Once you know what it means, you can actually recycle it properly. Most people never flip the container over. ## Caption 2

Pop quiz: what does the number 6 mean on a plastic container? It means polystyrene. Every plastic product is stamped with a resin identification code from 1 to 7. Most people see the triangle and assume it means "recyclable," but it doesn't. It just tells you the material. And when you know the material, you know how to handle it. Number 6 polystyrene is recyclable. You just need to know where to take it.

## Caption 3

Flip your container over. See that triangle with a number in it? That's not a recycling symbol. It's a material ID. Number 6 is polystyrene, one of the most common materials in food packaging. Foam cups, clamshell containers, meat trays, egg cartons. All #6. The reason most of it ends up in landfills isn't because it can't be recycled. It's because people don't know what it is.

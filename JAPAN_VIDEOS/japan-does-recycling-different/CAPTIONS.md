## Caption 1

Japan has a separate bin just for polystyrene. Not "plastics." Not "recyclables." Just polystyrene. Every neighborhood has a pickup day for it. Residents wash it, break it down, and set it out, and over 90% of it actually gets recycled. The US doesn't do this, not because it's impossible, but because we never built the system. Japan proved it works. Now it's our turn.

A 90%+ recycling rate for polystyrene. Compare that to the US, where most programs reject it entirely. It's not a technology problem. It's an infrastructure problem. And Japan already solved it.
